# Build Prompt — v0.4a: Strategy abstraction + three pre-registered long-only strategies

**For the coding session.** Read `CLAUDE.md` first; its prohibitions/invariants bind.
Build the smallest working spike for this scope. This is **step 1 of 2**: the abstraction

## Mission

Introduce a **minimal** strategy abstraction so the lab can run any of a small,
**pre-registered** set of strategies through the existing single-day (`run`) and range
(`run-range`) pipeline, and register **three distinct long-only strategies**:
`orb_long_5m` (existing, refactored), `vwap_reclaim_long`, `or_fade_long`.

## Guardrails (this feature edges near forbidden territory — hold the line)
- **Long-only.** No shorting/leverage (CLAUDE.md). Every strategy enters long only.
- **Distinct pre-registered hypotheses, NOT parameter variants.** Implement each rule
  **as specified below** and **do NOT tune thresholds to make them profitable** — that is
  the optimizer/grid-search trap the lab forbids. Expect most to die under friction.
- **Minimal abstraction:** a `TradePlan` + a tiny name→class registry. **Not** a plugin
  framework, not config-driven strategy wiring beyond a name.
- **Reuse, don't fork:** the no-lookahead firewall, pessimistic/naive fills, friction
  sweep, and verdict are strategy-agnostic mechanics — reuse them unchanged.
- No live/continuous running, no LLM, no portfolio/compounding. Determinism preserved.

## Scope

### 1. `TradePlan` (replaces/extends `EntrySignal`)
A strategy emits, on the bar that fires, a long entry plan the fill engine can price
generically:
```python
@dataclass(frozen=True)
class TradePlan:
    signal_bar_ts: datetime
    trigger_price: float      # the close that fired
    stop_price: float         # strategy-defined, must be < trigger (long)
    target_r_multiple: float  # target = entry + R*(entry - stop_price)  [FIX #2 geometry, generalized]
```
The pessimistic/naive engine keeps its exact mechanics (next-bar entry
`max(next_open, trigger)+slippage`, stop-wins-ambiguous, target-must-exceed, gap-through,
flatten); it just takes `stop_price`/`target_r_multiple` from the plan instead of deriving
them from the opening range.

### 2. Generalize the orchestration
- `strategy.on_bar(view) -> TradePlan | None` is the protocol. Strategies read **only** a
  `ReplayView` (firewall unchanged).
- `run_replay` / `run_one_day` take a **strategy instance/name** instead of hardcoding
  `OrbLongStrategy`. Refactor `orb_long.py` to emit a `TradePlan` (stop = OR low,
  `target_r_multiple` from config) — **its fills/P&L/verdict on the sample fixture must be
  unchanged** (regression).
- **Registry** in `strategy/__init__.py`: `STRATEGIES = {name: class}` for the three names.
  A helper `get_strategy(name)` raises clearly on an unknown name.

### 3. CLI
`run` and `run-range` gain `--strategy <name>` (default `orb_long_5m` for back-compat).
The report records the **strategy name** (replace the hardcoded `"orb_long_5m"`).

### 4. Report generalization (keep minimal)
Carry `strategy` (the registered name) in the report. The ORB-specific `opening_range`
field becomes optional strategy **context**: ORB populates `{or_high, or_low}`; the others
may expose their own small context dict or null. It is **fine if ORB's `report_hash`
changes** due to the added `strategy` field — keep it deterministic; do **not** chase the
old committed real-day snapshots (those are historical).

### 5. The three strategies (implement the rules AS WRITTEN — pre-registered, do not tune)
- **`orb_long_5m`** (existing): first 5 RTH bars = opening range; a later bar closing above
  OR high → long; stop = OR low. Refactor to `TradePlan`, behavior unchanged.
- **`vwap_reclaim_long`**: maintain the **cumulative session VWAP** from *completed* bars
  only (`Σ vwap_i·vol_i / Σ vol_i`; no lookahead). After a short warmup (e.g. the first 5
  RTH bars), trigger long when a bar **closes back above** the cumulative VWAP having closed
  **below** it on the prior bar (a reclaim). stop = the lowest low since session open (or
  the reclaim bar's low — pick one, fixed). One trade/day.
- **`or_fade_long`** (failed-breakdown reclaim): opening range = first 5 RTH bars. After
  the OR window, if a bar **closes below OR low** (breakdown) and a **later bar closes back
  above OR low** (reclaim/bear-trap) → long; stop = the lowest low of the breakdown swing
  (below OR low). One trade/day. Distinct from ORB (which is breakout *above* OR high).
